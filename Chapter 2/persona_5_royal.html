Persona 5 Royal: Story Explained

The Beginning – A New Life and The Metaverse

The protagonist, a high school student, is falsely accused of assault while trying to help a woman and is sentenced to probation. Sent to Tokyo, he moves in with Sojiro Sakura, a coffee shop owner, and enrolls at Shujin Academy. While trying to adjust to his new life, he befriends Ryuji Sakamoto, an outcast with a grudge against their abusive gym teacher, Suguru Kamoshida.

One day, they accidentally stumble into a mysterious alternate dimension known as the Metaverse, where people’s distorted desires take the form of Palaces—manifestations of their corrupt minds. In Kamoshida’s Palace, they meet Morgana, a talking cat-like creature who teaches them about Persona powers. The protagonist unlocks his Persona, Arsène, becoming the leader of a group that would later be known as the Phantom Thieves of Hearts.

With Ann Takamaki joining them, the trio successfully changes Kamoshida’s heart, forcing him to confess his crimes of abuse and harassment at Shujin Academy. This marks the beginning of their journey as the Phantom Thieves—changing the hearts of corrupt individuals and bringing justice where the law fails.

The Phantom Thieves’ Rise & Yaldabaoth’s Scheme

As the Phantom Thieves’ reputation grows, they recruit new members, each with their own personal struggles: Yusuke Kitagawa, an artist manipulated by his corrupt mentor; Makoto Niijima, a model student oppressed by authority; Futaba Sakura, a reclusive hacker dealing with trauma; and Haru Okumura, a corporate heiress whose father exploits his workers. They continue reforming corrupt individuals, targeting figures like mafia leader Kaneshiro, corrupt TV producer Okumura, and others.

However, their actions attract the attention of the public and the authorities, particularly Goro Akechi, a charismatic detective. Akechi initially joins them, but later reveals himself as a double agent working for Masayoshi Shido, a ruthless politician who orchestrated the protagonist’s false conviction. Akechi attempts to eliminate the Phantom Thieves but is ultimately betrayed by Shido and seemingly perishes.

Meanwhile, it is revealed that the Phantom Thieves’ activities were being manipulated by a higher power: Yaldabaoth, a god-like entity controlling society from the shadows. Yaldabaoth, disguised as the Velvet Room’s Igor, has been using the protagonist and Akechi in a cosmic game to determine the fate of humanity. With the public turning against the Phantom Thieves, they are forced to fight against Yaldabaoth in a climactic battle, ultimately defeating him and freeing society from his control.

Royal’s New Semester - The True Ending & End of a False Reality

In Persona 5 Royal, a new semester is introduced, revealing that reality has been altered. Dr. Maruki, a cognitive scientist, has gained control over the Metaverse and created an ideal world where everyone’s suffering is erased. Ryuji regains his lost track career, Futaba’s mother is alive, and Akechi is mysteriously back. However, this reality is built on illusions, taking away free will.

The protagonist must choose whether to accept this world or fight to restore reality. The Phantom Thieves reunite and battle Maruki, who, despite his good intentions, ultimately understands that true happiness cannot be forced upon people. After an emotional fight, they manage to change his heart, restoring the real world.

With their journey complete, the protagonist’s probation ends, and he prepares to leave Tokyo. However, if certain choices are made, Akechi’s fate remains ambiguous. The game ends with the protagonist and his friends moving forward, forever changed by their experiences.
About Persona 5 Royal

Persona 5 Royal is a JRPG game developed by the company ATLUS, and is the widely successful successor to their previous game, Persona 4 Golden. Follow the Phantom Thieves of Hearts and their leader, Joker, as they navigate the corruption and lies of a world hidden from common man's view. Be their witness as they tackle the Metaverse and Palaces in their pursuit of their own freedom and justice.

Persona is a RPG series set in modern-day Japan that is acclaimed for its narrative style of confronting everyday realities such as forging friendships and romances, alongside supernatural and paranormal themes, including uncovering and solving mysterious rumors and various urban legends. The series typically follows a story that unfolds after a group of teenagers discover their abilities as Persona-users, chronicling the growth of their friendship and inner selves as they confront numerous obstacles. In recent years, the franchise has been adapted to animated TV series, animated films, music concerts, theatrical stages, manga, and continues to expand its influence.

“Persona” refers to the alternate persona that lies within you—another “you” who materializes themselves in form of mythical deities and demons possessing great power.

Characters

Protagonist

VA: Xander Mobus

A second-year high school student who moved in the spring and transfered to Shujin Academy. One day, with his back against the wall, his powers as a Persona-user awakens. He lives as a normal student in the day as a tenant of a café owned by a family friend. After school, he turns into a Phantom Thief who upheaves the city.

Kasumi Yoshizawa

VA: Laura Post

A beautiful girl who transfers to Shujin Academy the same spring as the protagonist. She's a talented gymnast since middle school and Shujin Academy holds high hopes for her because of her excellent athletic records.

Ryuji Sakamoto

VA: Max Mittelman

A fellow second-year student at Shujin Academy. Although his words and actions can be crude, he has a caring, dependable, and outgoing personality. Due to an accident, he is labeled as a delinquent and fellow students began distancing themselves from him. After befriending the recently transferred protagonist, he awakens his abilities as a Persona-user.

Morgana

VA: Cassandra Lee Morris

A mysterious cat-like creature that the protagonist and Ryuji encounter in the Metaverse, he decides to accompany them shortly after. He can communicate with humans and claims to be able to transform into various objects, yet many things about him remain shrouded in mystery. Although he can be aggressive at times, he's also incredibly selfless and supports his teammates. He often claims "I'm not a cat!"

Ann Takamaki

VA: Erika Harlacher

A fellow second-year student at Shujin Academy, and a classmate to the protagonist. She's an attractive girl who is one quarter American and models on the side. She's free-spirited and has an honest personality, but due to her striking appearance, she's isolated from everyone around her. After befriending the protagonist, an incident acts as a catalyst to awaken her abilities as a Persona-user.

Yusuke Kitagawa

VA: Matthew Mercer

A second-year student at Kosei High School who specializes in painting. His attractive appearance draws attention, but his unique artistic sensibilities often lead him out of touch with others. He's a live-in apprentice for Ichiryusei Madarame, a master artist. A turn of events leads him to befriend the protagonist and awaken his abilities as a Persona-user.
